# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Atrium is primarily for AI-native technical and product teams who collaborate with people and long-running coding, research, and production agents as peers. They move repeatedly between discussion, delegation, live progress, intervention, review, and artifacts. They need a shared environment that remains understandable when many human and agent actors are working at once.

## Product Purpose

Atrium is the shared place where teams direct, observe, and build with agents in real time. It keeps conversation, sessions, decisions, files, changes, and resulting artifacts connected so a small team can coordinate work with the capability of a much larger one. Success means users can move through the primary collaboration loop without losing context, control, or trust.

## Positioning

Work with agents together.

## Brand Personality

Calm, capable, alive. Atrium should feel focused and trustworthy without becoming sterile: active work is visibly present, collaboration feels human, and complex agent activity remains legible. The desired emotional outcome is amplification—the sense that the team can take on materially more together—supported by control rather than spectacle.

Linear and Slack are interaction references, not visual templates: Linear for precision, keyboard fluency, and resolved states; Slack for shared presence, conversational legibility, and durable team context.

## Anti-references

Atrium must not feel like a generic AI dashboard. Avoid decorative gradients, interchangeable card grids, vague agent-magic language, ornamental glass, gratuitous “AI” glow, and controls that imply capability without making work inspectable. It must also avoid reducing agents to bots bolted onto ordinary chat.

## Design Principles

1. **Keep work connected.** Conversation, delegation, progress, decisions, changes, and artifacts should read as parts of one collaboration loop.
2. **Make agency legible.** Users should understand who or what is acting, what state the work is in, what changed, and where intervention is possible.
3. **Amplify without overwhelming.** Support dense, parallel work while preserving a clear next action and a calm hierarchy.
4. **Earn every surface.** Add structure, decoration, motion, or controls only when they improve orientation, trust, or throughput.
5. **One product, native behavior.** Equivalent concepts should remain coherent across platforms while respecting web, desktop, iOS, and Android conventions.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA throughout and exceed it in core workflows. Keyboard navigation, focus order, screen-reader semantics, contrast, font scaling, high-contrast preferences, reduced motion, touch targets, and recovery messaging are product requirements rather than polish. Native surfaces must also honor platform accessibility settings and interaction conventions.
